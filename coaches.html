Coach at Day of Code!


Day of Code is inspired by RailsGirls, a format which has proved successful at taking people brand new to code and providing them with a useful taster day in a safe, inspiring environment. The format works by having small groups of delegates following detailed technical guides, supported by friendly coaches who feel knowledgeable enough to be pretty confident on the command line, able to read documentation and field questions from delegates. We're excited that our coaches include some of the most prominent and enthusiastic publisher-programmers in the book trade today. And if you'd like to join them, that's great: enthusiasm and interest is the most important prerequisite for being a coach.

Day of Code is your chance to encourage publishing people who think that coding might be inaccessible to them, and to help shatter the illusion that coding is impossible, or 'not for people like me'.

What do I need to be a coach?

  • Patience, willingness to help, and a friendly attitude
  • Adherence to our Code of Conduct
  • Being confident enough to field all sorts of questions in a beginner-friendly way throughout the duration of the workshop, bearing in mind that sitting and Googling together is a great way to show how most programming works! You're not meant to know everything; your role is to calmly provide insights into how to find things out
  • Experience with web development helps, but any programming experience will be great
  • Some time to go through the tutorial before the event.
